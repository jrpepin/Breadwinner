Breadwinner Estimates (SIPP 2014)

Data

This analysis uses data from the 2014 Survey of Income and Program Participation. We use all 4 waves of the survey data, which spans January 2013 to December 2016. The total sample consists of 82464 individuals.

The description of the variables can be found in the SIPP 2014 Wave 4 Metadata report. We use the following variables for this analysis.

Sample

We restricted the sample to 8462 women who first became mothers within the 25 years prior to each interview and currently coreside with minor (age < 18) children.
We use the birth history measures (year of each birth) to identify the sample eligble to be included in our analysis.

Measures

We created a measure of household earnings by aggregating the earnings across all type 1 individuals in the household in each month and then aggregating earnings across all months in each year to get an annual measure of household earnings.

To create indicators of breadwinning we compare each mothers’ annual earnings to the annual household earnings measures. We consider mothers who earn more than 50% (or 60%) of the household earnings breadwinners.

Results

We observe 24.1% breadwining, or earning more than 50% of the household income in the year of their first birth. This estimate is lower (16.7 percent) using a 60% threshold.

The percentage never breadwinning by the time their first child reaches age 18 is 7.7, or 15.7 using the 60% threshold.

The percentage breadwinning by the time their first child reaches age 18 is 92.3% or 84.3% using the 60% threshold.
