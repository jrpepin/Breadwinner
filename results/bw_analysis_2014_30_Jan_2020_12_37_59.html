Breadwinner Estimates (SIPP 2014)

Data

This analysis uses data from the 2014 Survey of Income and Program Participation. We use all 4 waves of the survey data, which spans January 2013 to December 2016. The total sample consists of 82464 individuals.

The description of the variables can be found in the SIPP 2014 Wave 4 Metadata report. We use the following variables for this analysis.

Measures

We created a measure of household earnings by aggregating the earnings across all type 1 individuals in the household in each month and then aggregating earnings across all months in each year to get an annual measure of household earnings.

To create indicators of breadwinning we compare each mothers’ annual earnings to the annual household earnings measures. We consider mothers who earn more than 50% (or 60%) of the household earnings breadwinners.

Results

We observe 22.3% breadwining, or earning more than 50% of the household income in the year of their first birth. This estimate is lower (15.4 percent) using a 60% threshold.

The percentage never breadwinning by the time their first child reaches age 18 is 13.0, or 19.9 using the 60% threshold.

The percentage breadwinning by the time their first child reaches age 18 is 87.0% or 80.1% using the 60% threshold.

This is likely an overestimate because many women probably previously breadwon prior to our initial observation and, as we explain in the paper, including those who breadwon at earlier duration upwardly biases the estimate of risk of transitioning into breadwinning at later durations. We can address this by limiting te estimate of transitions to later waves among women not observed breadwinning in earlier waves.
